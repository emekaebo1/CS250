# CS250
Throughout this course, I learned that understanding user needs begins with listening to stakeholders and translating their goals into clear, actionable requirements. One of the most effective ways to do this is by creating user stories, which describe a feature from the user's perspective and explain why it is valuable. User stories help developers, testers, and Product Owners maintain a shared understanding of the project's objectives while breaking large requirements into smaller, manageable tasks. They also encourage ongoing communication with stakeholders, allowing teams to gather feedback and refine features throughout development instead of waiting until the project is complete.
I also learned that developing software is most effective when it is approached as an iterative process rather than a single, linear effort. In the future, I hope to incorporate Agile practices such as sprint planning, daily Scrum meetings, continuous testing, backlog refinement, and sprint retrospectives because these processes promote collaboration, adaptability, and continuous improvement. Being a good team member in software development means communicating openly, supporting teammates, accepting feedback, and taking ownership of assigned work while remaining flexible when requirements change. By working together and focusing on delivering value to users, Agile teams can produce higher-quality software that better meets customer needs.
